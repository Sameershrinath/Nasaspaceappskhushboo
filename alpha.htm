🌠 Alpha Centauri — Our Closest Star Neighbor 

 
    The closest group of stars to us  is called  Alpha Centauri — it’s like our Sun’s friendly neighbor in the universe! 🪐✨

     Only about 4.37 light-years away.

      That means light from Alpha Centauri takes just over 4 years to reach us — super close in space terms!

🌟 A Family of Three Star


    Alpha Centauri isn’t just one star — it’s actually a triple star system made up of three stars:

Alpha Centauri A – a bright, yellow star like our Sun (but a bit bigger and hotter).

Alpha Centauri B – a smaller, orange star, slightly cooler than the Sun.

Proxima Centauri – a tiny, red dwarf star, and the closest star to Earth (even closer than the other two!).

🪐 Proxima Centauri and Its Planets


    Scientists have discovered planets orbiting Proxima Centauri:

Proxima b – a rocky planet that’s almost the size of Earth! It lies in the “habitable zone,” where liquid water might exist.

Proxima c – a larger planet farther out, likely too cold for life.

This makes Proxima Centauri one of the most exciting places in the search for alien life! 👽
