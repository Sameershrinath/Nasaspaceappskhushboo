Stars of Our Solar System

Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our Solar System, providing light and heat to all planets.

Sirius

Sirius is the brightest star in the night sky, part of the Canis Major constellation.

Alpha Centauri

Alpha Centauri is the closest star system to our Solar System, consisting of three stars.

Altair

Altair is the brightest star in the constellation Aquila and is one of the closest stars visible to the naked eye.

Betelgeuse

Betelgeuse is a bright red supergiant star in the Orion constellation, nearing the end of its life.

Deneb

Deneb is one of the most luminous stars known, located in the constellation Cygnus.

Polaris

Polaris, also known as the North Star, is located almost directly above Earth's North Pole and has been used for navigation for centuries.

Vega

Vega is the brightest star in the constellation Lyra and one of the closest stars to Earth.
